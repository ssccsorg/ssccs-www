# SSCCS Foundation (In Formation)

## I. Establishment, Name, and Legal Status

1.  The entity hereby constituted shall be known as “SSCCS Foundation
    (in formation)”.
2.  The Foundation is established as an independent, non-profit legal
    entity and shall acquire legal personality upon incorporation or
    registration in accordance with the laws of the jurisdiction in
    which such incorporation or registration is effected.
3.  The registered office of the Foundation shall be determined upon
    incorporation and may be transferred in accordance with applicable
    law.
4.  The Foundation is constituted exclusively for public-benefit
    purposes as set forth herein.

## II. Objects

1.  The objects of the Foundation are to promote and advance:
    1.  science and research;
    2.  education; and
    3.  environmentally sustainable and energy-efficient technological
        development.
2.  In furtherance of these objects, the Foundation may, without
    limitation:
    1.  conduct, support, and disseminate scientific research, including
        research relating to the Scheme-Segment Structure-based,
        Composition and Observation-driven Computing System (SSCCS) and
        related computational frameworks;
    2.  develop, publish, and maintain open-source software and
        technical documentation;
    3.  prepare and distribute academic publications, instructional
        materials, and research reports;
    4.  facilitate cooperation and exchange among researchers,
        institutions, and organizations internationally;
    5.  organize and support conferences, seminars, workshops, and
        related educational activities;
    6.  establish and manage intellectual property rights, including
        copyrights and patents, in a manner consistent with the
        Foundation’s non-profit and public-benefit purposes;
    7.  adopt, utilize, and promote permissive open-source licenses for
        software developed by or contributed to the Foundation,
        including but not limited to the Apache 2.0 License, and shall
        hold or manage any associated copyrights or ownership interests
        to ensure the licensed software remains perpetually free and
        open;
    8.  undertake such other lawful activities as are incidental or
        conducive to the attainment of the foregoing objects.
3.  The Foundation shall not carry on activities that are inconsistent
    with its non-profit character or public-benefit purpose.

## III. Non-Profit Character and Application of Assets

1.  The Foundation shall operate exclusively on a non-profit basis.
2.  The income and assets of the Foundation shall be applied solely
    toward the promotion of its objects as set forth in this Charter and
    Statute.
3.  No part of the income or assets of the Foundation shall be
    distributed, directly or indirectly, to any founder, governing body
    member, contributor, or private individual.
4.  The payment of reasonable remuneration for services actually
    rendered and the reimbursement of properly documented expenses shall
    not be deemed a distribution of profits.
5.  The Foundation shall not be organized or operated for the primary
    purpose of carrying on a trade or business for profit.

## IV. Governance

1.  The Foundation shall be governed by such bodies as are established
    upon incorporation in accordance with applicable law, including at
    minimum a governing body responsible for strategic oversight and an
    executive body responsible for management and representation.
2.  The composition, appointment, removal, powers, duties, and terms of
    office of members of such bodies shall be determined in accordance
    with applicable law and further specified in internal regulations or
    bylaws adopted pursuant thereto.
3.  All governing bodies shall exercise their functions in good faith,
    with due care, and in a manner consistent with the Foundation’s
    objects and non-profit status.
4.  No governance arrangement shall authorize the diversion of the
    Foundation’s assets from its stated objects.

## V. Financial Provisions

1.  The Foundation shall maintain accurate books and records of account
    and shall prepare financial statements in accordance with applicable
    law.
2.  Any surplus arising from the activities of the Foundation shall be
    retained and applied solely toward its objects.
3.  The Foundation may establish reserves or endowment funds to ensure
    its long-term sustainability, provided that such reserves are
    consistent with applicable law and the Foundation’s non-profit
    character.
4.  The financial resources of the Foundation shall be managed with due
    diligence and prudence.

## VI. Dissolution and Asset Distribution

1.  The Foundation may be dissolved in accordance with applicable law.
2.  Upon dissolution, and after satisfaction of all lawful liabilities,
    the remaining assets of the Foundation shall be transferred to one
    or more organizations that:
    1.  are organized and operated exclusively for public-benefit or
        non-profit purposes; and
    2.  pursue objects substantially similar to those of the Foundation.
3.  In no event shall any remaining assets be distributed to private
    individuals or for private benefit.

## VII. Interpretation and Effect

1.  This Foundational Charter and Statute shall take effect upon the
    lawful incorporation or registration of the Foundation.
2.  The provisions of this document shall be interpreted so as to
    preserve, to the greatest extent permitted by applicable law, the
    non-profit and public-benefit character of the Foundation.
3.  If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable under applicable
    law, such invalidity shall not affect the remaining provisions,
    which shall remain in full force and effect.
4.  This document constitutes the foundational and governing instrument
    of the Foundation, subject to mandatory provisions of applicable
    law.
